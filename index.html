Research Focus

Two Pillars of Inquiry

01 / Experimental
🪨

High-Stress Rock Mechanics Experiments

Conducting state-of-the-art laboratory experiments under extreme pressure and temperature conditions that simulate deep geothermal environments. These tests probe how rocks fracture, deform, and transmit fluids at the microscale — generating ground-truth data that informs reservoir-scale understanding.

Triaxial Testing Fracture Mechanics High-P/T Conditions Permeability
02 / Numerical
🌐

Reservoir-Scale Flow Simulations

Developing and running high-fidelity numerical models of fluid flow behavior in geothermal reservoirs. By coupling experimental observations with computational frameworks, the goal is to reliably predict subsurface flow dynamics, heat extraction efficiency, and long-term reservoir performance.

About

Background &
Motivation

I am a PhD student at the Earth Mechanics and Physics for Energy Sustainability (EMES) Lab at Virginia Tech's Department of Geosciences, where I joined in January 2025 under the supervision of Professor Mateo Acosta.

My research sits at the intersection of experimental rock physics and computational geomechanics. A central challenge in geothermal energy is the scale gap — the physics governing fluid flow and deformation at the grain scale are vastly different from what we observe at field scale. I work to close this gap.

By combining laboratory measurements with upscaled numerical models, I aim to build a more complete physical picture of how geothermal reservoirs behave — ultimately supporting more efficient and sustainable energy extraction from the Earth's heat.

Toolkit

Methods & Approaches

⚙️

Rock Deformation Testing

Triaxial and true-triaxial apparatus for probing failure envelopes and elastic properties under simulated in-situ conditions.

🔬

Microstructural Analysis

SEM, CT imaging, and microscopy to characterize pore networks, fracture geometry, and mineralogy at the grain scale.

💧

Permeability Measurement

Steady-state and transient flow experiments to quantify how stress and fractures control fluid transmissibility.

🖥️

Numerical Simulation

Finite element and finite volume models for coupled hydro-mechanical flow simulation at reservoir scale.

📐

Upscaling Methods

Translating micro- to macroscale properties using homogenization theory and representative volume element analysis.

🌋

Geothermal Systems

Application domain: enhanced geothermal systems (EGS), hydrothermal reservoirs, and deep heat extraction strategies.
